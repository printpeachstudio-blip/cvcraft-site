How Much Does a Professional CV Rewrite Cost in the UK?

5 min read April 2026 By CVCraft AI

CV writing services in the UK range from around £9 to well over £500. That's a huge spread — and it's not always obvious what you actually get at each price point, or whether paying for a rewrite is genuinely worth it. This guide gives you an honest breakdown.

The UK CV writing market in 2026

The market for professional CV help has changed significantly in recent years. AI tools have driven prices down at the lower end, while established career consultants and executive CV specialists continue to charge premium rates for a more personal service.

There's also a lot of variation in quality at every price point. A £200 service isn't automatically better than a £30 one — and a £10 service isn't automatically a waste of money. What matters is what you're getting and whether it fits your situation.

What you get at different price points

Free tools and templates

£0

Canva, CV Library, Reed, and various online tools offer free CV templates and builders. These are fine for formatting but won't improve your content. They also tend to produce heavily templated CVs that look identical to thousands of others — which is a problem when recruiters see the same layout dozens of times a day.

Best for: Someone who just needs a clean layout and is confident writing strong content themselves.

AI-assisted CV rewriting services

£9 — £30

A growing category of services use AI to rewrite and optimise CVs at low cost. Quality varies significantly. The best of them — particularly those built specifically for CV writing rather than general AI tools — can produce genuinely strong results: proper ATS optimisation, keyword matching to the job description, and coherent professional language. The worst produce generic output that reads like it was written by a machine, which it was.

The key differentiator at this price point is whether the service tailors output to a specific job description or just produces a generic rewrite.

Best for: Job seekers on a budget who want ATS optimisation and a significant content improvement without the cost of a human consultant.

Freelance CV writers

£50 — £150

Platforms like Fiverr and PeoplePerHour have many freelance CV writers at this price point. Again, quality varies enormously. The best have HR or recruitment backgrounds and will ask detailed questions before writing. The worst will produce a lightly edited version of what you gave them and call it a rewrite. Reading reviews carefully and asking for samples before committing is essential at this tier.

Best for: Mid-career professionals who want a human touch but don't want to spend hundreds of pounds.

Professional CV writing services

£150 — £300

Established services like TopCV and The CV Centre operate in this range. They typically offer a consultation process, multiple revisions, and a more structured approach. They also tend to have dedicated writers for different sectors, so your CV is handled by someone with relevant industry knowledge. The price reflects genuine human expertise and time investment.

Best for: Senior professionals, executives, or career changers who want thorough human involvement and are comfortable with the cost.

Executive career consultants

£300 — £500+

At this level you're typically getting a comprehensive package: CV rewrite, LinkedIn profile optimisation, interview coaching, and ongoing career strategy support. The CV itself is one component of a broader engagement. For C-suite candidates targeting roles that pay £100k+, this investment can make sense. For most job seekers, it doesn't.

Best for: Senior executives, board-level candidates, or those making a high-stakes career transition where every detail matters.

Is it worth paying for a CV rewrite?

The honest answer: it depends on what the alternative costs you.

If you're applying for a role that pays £35,000 a year and you've been out of work for two months, the cost of that unemployment is already £5,800 in lost earnings. A £10 or £50 investment in a better CV is trivially small by comparison.

The question isn't really "can I afford a CV rewrite?" — it's "can I afford not to fix this if my current CV isn't working?"

A CV that consistently gets rejected is costing you far more in lost salary than any rewrite service charges. The maths almost always favour investing in fixing it.

What to look for when choosing a service

  • Tailoring to the job description — a generic rewrite is much less valuable than one that's been optimised for the specific role you're applying for
  • ATS optimisation — does the service understand how applicant tracking systems work and actively format and keyword-optimise for them?
  • Turnaround time — how quickly will you receive your document?
  • Revision policy — what happens if you're not happy with the result?
  • Sample work or reviews — can you see examples of previous output or read genuine customer reviews?
